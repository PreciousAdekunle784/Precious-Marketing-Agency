The Real Problem

Why Most Google Ads Campaigns
Quietly Bleed Money

Running Google Ads is not difficult. Running them profitably is what most businesses never crack. Not because the platform does not work. Because of the same four silent killers in almost every unmanaged account.

💵
Broad Match Keywords Burning Budget on People Who Will Never Buy
Without tight match types and a maintained negative keyword list, Google serves your ad to job seekers, students, and competitors. In a typical unmanaged account, 40 to 60% of spend goes to searches that could never convert. The money leaves. The leads do not arrive.
🚨
Paying for Clicks That Land on a Page Built to Lose
The ad got the click. The landing page had no clear offer, no urgency, and three competing calls to action. The visitor left. The money did not come back. Most campaigns lose 70% of their value at the landing page, not the ad.
📈
Running Ads Without Verified Conversion Tracking
Without verified tracking, Google's algorithm optimises for the wrong signals, scales campaigns that cost money, and ignores the ones generating leads. Data-blind campaigns multiply every other mistake by ten. This is non-negotiable: tracking first.
👻
An Agency That Touches Your Account Once a Month If You Are Lucky
Google Ads is a live auction. Competitor bids shift hourly. Trends change daily. An account reviewed monthly is an account losing ground every single day. This is the most common reason Google Ads fails. Not the platform. The absence of daily management.
